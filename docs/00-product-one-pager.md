# Gulp — Product One-Pager

*Internal north-star · v1 · 2026-06-18*

> **Positioning —** Gulp is the **personal learning system for the age of information overload**: it digests everything you consume into knowledge you can recall, test, and reuse.
>
> **Tagline —** *Forward anything. Gulp turns it into knowledge you can actually remember.*

---

## Why this matters

The smarter AI gets, the more it does *for* us — and the less we do for ourselves. The convenient path is quietly costly: hand the thinking over often enough, and your own judgment, memory, and understanding start to fade. We drift into working *for* the AI instead of the other way around.

Gulp is built on the opposite conviction: **AI's highest use is to make the person using it stronger.** Same powerful models — pointed at your growth, not just your output. You stay the one who understands; AI just gets you there faster.

## The insight — the unowned chain

Today's tools solve the wrong half of the problem: they help us **save** and **find**, never **understand**, **remember**, or **use**. Saving ≠ learning.

The value chain splits into three layers, and **no one owns all three**:

- **Intake** — Feedly, Inoreader, Readwise Reader, Cubox, Matter, Raindrop
- **Digest** — Recall, NotebookLM, Mem, Tana, Notion AI, ChatGPT
- **Mastery** — Anki, RemNote, SuperMemo, Quizlet

Recall comes closest but is weak on source management, the daily knowledge stream, and a real mastery model. The end-to-end loop — *source → AI digestion → knowledge base → conversation capture → active mastery* — is unowned. **That gap is the opening.**

## What Gulp is

One product that collapses the three layers into a single loop:

1. **Intake** — a universal inbox for anything you consume.
2. **Digest** — AI turns each input into a *knowledge pack*, not just a summary.
3. **Mastery** — knowledge becomes cards, tested and scheduled until you actually own it.

The name is the thesis: you **gulp** information in; the system digests it, absorbs it, and makes you review it.

## The core loop (north-star flow)

> **Forward anything → Gulp builds a knowledge pack → You spend 5 minutes in Gulp mode → The system tracks what you've mastered.**

Every pass through this loop should leave the user measurably closer to *knowing* something — not just to *having saved* it.

## Who it's for

Curious knowledge workers and lifelong learners who consume far more than they retain — the people who save 50 links a week, read 5, and remember 1. **Initial wedge:** Chinese-language power-consumers (WeChat-forward, browser-extension, share-sheet habits) who feel the overload most acutely and have no end-to-end tool today.

## MVP scope — the sharp loop

Ship one sharp loop, **not** a full PKM. Six features:

1. **Universal inbox** — links, articles, PDFs, YouTube, podcasts, audio, screenshots, notes. China-first capture: WeChat forward, browser extension, share sheet, email-in.
2. **AI knowledge pack** — per input: summary, background, key terms, people/orgs, core claims, counter-views, links to existing knowledge.
3. **Card-based knowledge base** — decompose content into typed objects: **Source · Claim · Concept · Question · Card · Conversation · Insight**.
4. **Gulp mode** — a daily 5–10 min learning stream: new knowledge, due reviews, retests of misses, short-answer, MCQ, explain-it, apply-it, "say it in your own words."
5. **Conversation capture** — chat about any card; on exit, Gulp extracts new points, corrected misconceptions, and questions to review.
6. **Mastery + scheduling** — start simple (right / wrong / fuzzy → adjust interval); evolve toward FSRS later.

### Non-goals (v1) — what we refuse to build

- Not a general-purpose PKM / second brain (no graphs to wire, no folders to maintain).
- Not a read-it-later reader competing on reading UX.
- Not a team / collaboration product.
- Not a content platform — we manage *your* stream, not curated courses.
- No manual-notes-first workflow — we start from the real information stream.

## Principles

1. **Digestion, not collection.** Saving is table stakes; understanding is the product.
2. **Mastery is the unit, not the bookmark.** Every concept has a state — from *unread* to *mastered* to *at risk of forgetting*.
3. **Forward anything.** Capture must be effortless, or the loop never starts.
4. **The system builds itself.** Users don't architect a knowledge base — Gulp generates the structure and the learning path.
5. **Conversations are assets.** Every AI chat produces knowledge, not history.

## What success looks like

The one signal: **users return to Gulp mode without being forced**, and their measured mastery — cards graduated, retention over time — climbs week over week. If people *learn* from Gulp instead of just *stashing* in it, we're winning.
